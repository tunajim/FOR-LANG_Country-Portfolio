Post 8: Security Issue: Terrorism

A Northern Threat

Terrorism in South Korea is rare, in fact the primary threat of terrorism comes from North Korea. However, most incidents of terrorism have occurred outside of the Korean Peninsula. These typically have been assassination attempts upon South Korean Presidents, but they also include the kidnapping of fisherman and bombings.1 The consequences of terrorism in South Korea are harsh, anyone convicted of being a 'ringleader' will be sentenced to death.2

Despite terrorism being relatively rare, the situation in South Korea remains particularly tense, considering North Korea's willingness to make use of nuclear weapons. Very recently, in October of 2023, they threatened the use of nukes in response to a United States Aircraft carrier being docked in South Korea.3 This breeds an environment of constant anxiety, where peace might be shattered at any moment.
