Post 3: Social Issue - Religion tension and tolerance

Religion in South Korea

South Korea has an open and accepting stance with regard to religions. Everyone is granted freedom of religion and their is a great deal of religious diversity in the country.1 There is separation of church and state with no state-sponsored religion. Buddhism was originally introduced to the Korean Peninsula in 372 A.D. as a result of Korean monks traveling through regions in China and India.2 Today, roughly 16% of South Koreans are Buddhists, while about 21% are Christian.3 However, the majority of South Koreans do not belong to any religion at all. Overall, South Korea is extremely tolerant of all religions and it is a place where you can freely practice whatever beliefs you might hold.

Conclusion

As societal groups continue to merge and integrate with each other, the number of interacting religions will also increase. In the United States, there is legal freedom of religion, but it seems more freedom is given to certain ones. In this regard, South Korea is a model society for how to approach religious tolerance. This is made possible in part by the strict delineation between church and state, an idea becoming less and less understood within the States.
